Note on observed%:
  'observed' here = (hyper contacts ∩ surface pool) /
                    (all contacts ∩ surface pool) × 100.
  This is DIFFERENT from the unfiltered observed% reported by
  validate_af3_contacts.py (which uses (hyper contacts) /
  (all contacts), so the denominator includes contacts that
  fall on buried residues). Do NOT divide the surface-filtered
  observed by the classic uniform baseline — the two
  numerator/denominator pairs come from different pools and
  the ratio is methodologically meaningless.

  For the genuine classic baseline (uniform over whole
  sequence), refer to data/validation/reports/summary.txt
  produced by validate_af3_contacts.py.

Interpretation note:
  The surface-filtered (apo-SASA) baseline is the honest
  null for interchain contacts: residues that are buried
  even when the chain is isolated cannot physically form
  an interface. The classic uniform baseline includes
  such core residues and is therefore a useful but
  permissive reference.
